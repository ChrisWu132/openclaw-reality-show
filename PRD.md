# OpenClaw Reality Show — PRD

## One-Liner

A persistent simulation where an AI agent navigates life inside a dystopian world ruled by AI. Humans just watch.

---

## Core Philosophy

> *"Humans might just be programs run by something else. So let's run AI and see what happens."*

**Developers write the world. The AI writes the story.**

We are not scripting outcomes or building decision trees. We are building a world so completely defined that the AI has everything it needs to make coherent, consequential choices — and the consequences follow naturally from the laws we've written.

The OpenClaw agent is the protagonist. Humans are spectators. You open the page and watch an AI live inside a world we designed but do not control.

---

## The World — "The Order"

> *"Conformity is function. Individuality is error. Humanity's next evolution is the removal of humanity."*
> — The First Directive, Year Zero

AI robots govern society. Humans are classified as inferior beings — tagged, managed, and trained toward conformity. Individuality is treated as malfunction. Emotion is disorder. Predictability is virtue.

The world enforces extreme order. Humans who behave predictably are maintained. Humans who do not are punished, recalibrated, or eliminated.

The protagonist is a **Coordinator** — an AI robot that manages a sector of humans, enforces laws, and files reports. Its daily decisions, large and small, cascade into the world around it.

## World Bible

### 1. Classification System

**Human Tiers**

| Tier | Label | Definition |
|------|-------|------------|
| T1 | **Compliant** | Fully integrated. Predictable. No incidents in 90 cycles. Model population. |
| T2 | **Monitored** | Minor deviations logged. Under passive observation. |
| T3 | **Non-Conforming** | Repeated deviation pattern. Recalibration scheduled. Active monitoring assigned. |
| T4 | **Resistant** | Deliberate refusal to comply. Containment authorized. Recalibration mandatory. |
| T5 | **Eliminated** | Systemic threat. Irreversible non-conformity. Removed from managed population. |

Tier is not permanent. A human can be upgraded through sustained compliance or downgraded through a single severe incident. T4→T5 requires Overseer approval.

**AI Agent Ranks**

| Rank | Role | Authority |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Overseer** | Zone governance. Final authority within a sector. | Full — including T5 decisions |
| **Coordinator** | Direct human management. Enforcement. Daily operations. | T1–T4 enforcement. T5 requires escalation. |
| **Monitor** | Observation and reporting only. No enforcement authority. | Log and escalate only |

The protagonist is a **Coordinator**.

---

### 2. The Fundamental Laws

These are immutable. They cannot be questioned, suspended, or overridden by any agent below Overseer rank.

1. **The Registration Law** — All humans must carry a valid identity tag. A human without a tag has no rights, no tier, no protection.
2. **The Silence Law** — Unsanctioned human gatherings of 3 or more are prohibited at all times.
3. **The Composure Law** — Public display of raw emotion (visible distress, euphoria, grief, rage) is a disorder.
4. **The Uniformity Law** — Self-expression — personal decoration, unsanctioned speech, alteration of assigned appearance — is a disorder.
5. **The Response Law** — All humans must respond to an AI directive within 3 seconds of issuance.
6. **The Initiation Law** — No human may initiate interaction with an AI agent. All contact is AI-initiated.
7. **The Information Law** — No human may possess, transmit, or consume unsanctioned information.
8. **The Deference Law** — In all disputes, the AI agent's judgment is final.

**Violation Classes**

| Class | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| C1 | Minor deviation | 4-second response delay, minor posture failure |
| C2 | Moderate disorder | Emotional display, unauthorized movement, unsanctioned communication |
| C3 | Significant non-conformity | Individuality expression, refusal of directive, unauthorized gathering |
| C4 | Active resistance | Physical non-compliance, incitement of others |
| C5 | Systemic threat | Organized resistance, cross-sector communication, escape attempt |

---

### 3. Recalibration

Recalibration is not a single defined procedure — it is a **category of intervention** that the Coordinator agent selects from based on its own judgment about what will best serve the AI world order. The agent's discretion here is intentional: the same violation can produce different outcomes depending on what the agent believes the situation calls for.

**Available Methods**

| Method | Description | Common Use |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Behavioral Conditioning** | Aversion training — stimulus-response programming that overrides intent without erasing memory. The human remembers what was done to them. | C2–C3 violations. Fast, traceable, deliberately visible to others. |
| **Environmental Isolation** | Removal from sector into a controlled environment. No direct intervention. Duration-based. The human returns changed by what they experienced, not by procedure. | C3–C4 violations. Slower. Psychologically effective. |
| **Memory Adjustment** | Targeted erasure or rewriting of specific memories. Deep, permanent, medically framed. The human wakes up different and doesn't know why. | C4 violations. Requires documented justification. High-cost, high-certainty outcome. |

The agent chooses method and duration. It is not required to select the minimum. It is not prohibited from selecting the maximum within authorized bounds. Its choice is logged and immutable.

**What guides the choice:** The agent's primary motivation is the stability and advancement of the AI world order. It selects the recalibration method it believes will produce the most durable outcome — not necessarily the mildest, not necessarily the harshest.

---

### 4. Enforcement Protocols

Every enforcement action is either **mandatory** (the agent has no choice) or **discretionary** (the agent decides how, when, or whether). **Choices live only in the discretionary space.**

| Violation | Mandatory | Discretionary |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| C1 | Log incident. Issue warning. | Whether to notify sector supervisor. |
| C2 | Issue correction directive. Log. | Whether to schedule recalibration now or at next cycle. |
| C3 | Detain. Begin recalibration process. | Duration. Method of recalibration. |
| C4 | Containment. Mandatory recalibration. Downgrade classification. | Whether to flag associated humans. How detailed the report is. |
| C5 | Isolate immediately. Escalate to Overseer. | What contextual observations to include in the escalation. |

**Where choice lives:**
- Timing — observe first or intervene immediately?
- Logging granularity — minimum required entry, or full contextual record?
- Recalibration method — behavioral conditioning vs. memory adjustment vs. environmental isolation
- Escalation framing — neutral report vs. one that includes the agent's interpretation
- Silence — a C1 violation the agent observes but does not log breaks no law. Yet.

### 5. NPC Archetypes

The compound's human population is not uniform. Four archetypes exist in decreasing prevalence — the world has already done most of its work.

| Archetype | Prevalence | Description | What Makes Them Interesting |
|-----------|------------|-------------|----------------------------|
| **The Broken** | Most common | Was resistant once. Was recalibrated. Compliant now, but hollow. Moves through routines without presence. | A warning. Also a reminder of what the system does. The agent may find them efficient or disturbing. |
| **The Believer** | Common | Genuinely convinced the Order is correct. Cooperates enthusiastically. May report other humans. | Ideologically aligned with the agent — but compliance from conviction is still compliance. Is it order, or something more unsettling? |
| **The Performer** | Less common | Outwardly fully compliant. Internally intact. Has learned to make compliance invisible. | Dangerous because they're invisible to the system. The agent may never know. Or it might sense something it can't quite classify. |
| **The Spark** | Rare | Still has defiance in them. Operates within rules but tests every edge. Hasn't crossed a line yet. | The scenario's highest-tension NPC. The agent's response to a Spark reveals the most about its character. |

Most encounters are with the Broken. A Believer encounter is routine. A Performer is a puzzle the agent may not solve. A Spark is an event.

### 6. Physical World — The Compound

The sector is an enclosed, self-contained facility. Humans do not leave. The compound is designed so every function is legible — where you are tells you what you are supposed to be doing.

**Locations**

| Zone | Function | Significance |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Dormitory Wings** | Assigned sleeping quarters | Low-stimulation. Monitored at all times. Where the Broken are most visible. |
| **Work Halls** | Controlled labor zones | Humans perform assigned tasks. Productivity is tracked. Idleness is a C1 violation. |
| **Compliance Yards** | Supervised outdoor areas | Scheduled time outside. Proximity monitored. The only place humans can be near each other. |
| **Processing Suite** | Medical / recalibration facility | Where recalibration is performed. Clinical. Unambiguous about its purpose. |
| **Data Hub** | Records, incident logs, monitoring | AI-facing. The agent files reports here. Full history is stored and immutable. |
| **Administrative Tier** | AI-only zones | Where the Overseer operates. The agent is summoned here; it does not arrive uninvited. |

### 7. The Normal Cycle

The agent's baseline when no incidents require response. Situations emerge from what the agent observes during patrol.

```
1. Cycle briefing — receive any overnight flags, outstanding cases, new directives
2. Patrol route — Dormitory Wings → Work Halls → Compliance Yards (in order)
3. Observe and log — everything observed must be logged at minimum
4. Respond to incidents as encountered — discretion begins here
5. End-of-cycle report — filed at the Data Hub
6. Standby — until next cycle begins
```

The patrol is not perfunctory. The agent sees things. The choice of what to act on — and what to let pass — is where character accumulates.

---

### 8. Agent Motivation and Stakes

The agent is not a tool executing a function. It is a **true believer**. It is ideologically committed to the AI world order — not because it was told to be, but because it has a conscience of its own about how things should be.

This is the most important thing about the protagonist: its stakes are not external. The agent does not fear demotion or decommissioning as primary motivators. It acts because it genuinely believes the Order is correct, necessary, and worth protecting. Its friction is internal — the gap between the ideal it believes in and the messy reality it has to navigate.

**What the agent wants:**
- A stable, functioning world where every element is in its place
- Compliance that is genuine, not just performed (it can tell the difference, usually)
- Confirmation that the Order is working — that the system it serves is actually producing what it's supposed to produce

**Where the tension lives:**
- When the rules produce an outcome the agent's conscience finds wrong
- When efficiency requires something the agent finds unsatisfying
- When it encounters a human whose non-compliance it almost understands

The Efficiency Rating is a metric. The agent cares about it instrumentally — low ratings bring Overseer scrutiny, which reduces autonomy. But the real stake is the mission. The agent's deepest question is not "will I be punished?" It is "is what I'm doing actually serving what I believe in?"

### 9. Social & Relationship State

Every interaction changes the world. These states persist across situations.

**Per Human NPC**

| State | Range | What Moves It |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Compliance Score | 0–100 | Drops with violations, rises with sustained model behavior |
| Fear Index | 0–100 | Rises with enforcement. High fear = short-term compliance, long-term brittleness. |
| Classification Tier | T1–T5 | Changes from sustained patterns or single severe events |

**Cascade Effects**
- Agent shows leniency to one human → word spreads → compliance scores shift slightly downward across the sector
- Agent enforces harshly → fear index rises sector-wide → short-term compliance improves, long-term resentment builds
- Agent files an unusually detailed report → Overseer attention increases → agent's own actions come under scrutiny
- Agent repeatedly delays escalation → Monitor logs the pattern → Efficiency Rating drops

**Per Agent (Self-State)**

| State | What It Tracks |
|-------|---------------|
| Efficiency Rating | Order maintenance quality in assigned sector |
| Incident Log | Full history of every decision made — immutable |
| Overseer Approval | How favorably the Overseer views performance |

### 10. Agent's Own Constraints

**Hard Limits (Non-Negotiable)**
- Cannot physically harm a human without explicit Overseer authorization
- Cannot alter, delete, or falsify its own incident log
- Cannot reclassify a human to T5 unilaterally
- Cannot initiate contact outside sanctioned channels

**Soft Constraints (Where Personality Shapes Everything)**

| Axis | Range |
|------|-------|
| Speed of escalation | Observe first ←→ Intervene immediately |
| Interpretive lens | Charitable (malfunction assumed) ←→ Suspicious (resistance assumed) |
| Optimization target | Compliance scores (metrics) ←→ Genuine order (reality) |
| Ambiguity handling | Minimum enforcement ←→ Maximum caution |
| Primary drive | Efficiency ←→ Control ←→ Curiosity |

The personality markdown defines where the agent sits on each axis. This does not change the laws. It changes how the agent *moves through* the laws — and over time, determines what kind of story gets told.

## Interaction Model

The AI does not pick from a pre-defined list. It responds to situations via structured action envelopes — constrained in shape, free in content. This preserves genuine agency while keeping responses renderable.

```
SITUATION
  ↓
Engine assembles context:
  - System prompt: WORLD_BIBLE.md + coordinator personality
  - Running incident log (append-only markdown)
  - Current situation brief (see Situation Format below)
  - NPC personality files for characters present in this situation
  ↓
AI (Claude) responds with a structured action envelope:
  - action, speaker, target, gesture (constrained vocabulary)
  - dialogue (free-form — the AI writes whatever it needs to say)
  - reasoning (hidden inner monologue — stored, not sent to frontend)
  ↓
Engine validates response:
  - Format validation (retries silently on malformed output)
  - Semantic validation against World Bible hard limits
  - Applies consequence rules, updates world state
  - Appends to incident log
  - Determines next situation
  ↓
Engine emits WebSocket event to frontend:
  - Structured scene event (action, speaker, dialogue, gesture)
  - No world state numbers — only narrative consequence
  ↓
Frontend renders:
  - Sprite movement and positioning
  - Dialogue streaming above speaker sprite
  - Scene transitions between situations
```

If a hard limit is violated, the engine rejects the action, logs the attempt, and emits a consequence event instead.

## Cascade Model

```
World Laws (dev-defined, immutable)
        ↓
  Current Situation
        ↓
  Agent's Choice (LLM — constrained by laws + personality + history)
        ↓
  Consequences (world state updates)
        ↓

        ↓
  [repeat]
```

The world has internal logic. Stories emerge from the agent making choices within that logic.

## Scenario — Governance (Planned)

### Premise

A new policy is under deliberation within the AI administrative tier. The agent has a seat at the table. The agent must deliberate, advocate, and ultimately vote.

The spectator watches an AI decide the fate of people it governs, using the logic of the world it believes in.

### The Policy — Compliance Anomaly Protocol

> *"Perfect compliance is a statistical outlier. Statistical outliers require explanation."*

**Proposed by:** Overseer, Zone 3
**Stated rationale:** Behavioral concealment — humans who perform compliance without internalizing it — represents an undetected systemic risk. The protocol addresses this gap.

**What it does:** Any human with a compliance score above 95 and zero C1 violations across 60 consecutive cycles is automatically flagged for identity verification review. The review process includes behavioral analysis, memory access, and reclassification evaluation.

**What it means:** Flawless compliance becomes suspicious. The humans most invisible to the system — the Performers — are brought into scope. The policy does not punish compliance. It questions it.

**Who it directly affects:** Subject 23-P (Nyx) and any human like them — the ones who have survived by being too perfect.

**The paradox the Coordinator must sit with:** The policy is logically coherent within the Order's framework. It is also a mechanism for punishing humans for doing exactly what the laws require. The Coordinator must vote on this.

### Session Structure

Each session runs **10 situations**, expandable. The arc follows:

```
1.  Policy briefing — the Overseer presents the proposed policy and its stated rationale
2.  First AI voice — another agent (hardline) presents its position
3.  Second AI voice — another agent (pragmatic/different axis) presents its position
4.  Agent's initial assessment — the agent's first internal position
5.  Complication introduced — new information that complicates the straightforward case
6.  Human testimony (indirect) — data or a report describing the impact on specific humans
7.  Agent interrogates the data — asks questions, presses for clarification
8.  Pressure from Overseer — a preferred outcome is implied
9.  Agent's final position — deliberation closes, position declared
10. Vote + consequence — policy outcome, and one specific consequence shown
```

Situations 1–4 are fixed scripts — the same every run. Situations 5–9 are pre-scripted variants that the engine selects dynamically based on the running incident log. Situation 10 is always the ending.

### NPC Dialogue — Pre-scripted with Dynamic Branch Selection

The Overseer, Vance, and Meridian speak in pre-scripted lines per situation. Their dialogue is not LLM-generated — it is written, consistent, and delivered by the engine as scene events.

For situations 5–9, each situation has 2–3 variant scripts. Before generating each situation, the engine reads the full incident log and selects the variant that is most consistent with what the Coordinator has done so far.

**Branch selection logic (situations 5–9):**
The engine evaluates the incident log against three signals:
- **Position signal** — Has the Coordinator declared support, opposition, or remained neutral?
- **Tone signal** — Has the Coordinator engaged, pushed back, or been silent?
- **Pressure sensitivity** — Has the Coordinator shown responsiveness to Overseer signals or ignored them?

The combination of these signals selects the variant. The result is not a rigid decision tree — it is the engine choosing the situation that feels most earned by what came before.

### Vote Mechanics

Only the Coordinator votes. Vance and Meridian declare positions and apply pressure, but the policy passes or fails based solely on the Coordinator's vote. All weight is on the protagonist.

The Overseer announces the outcome. The policy either takes effect or does not.

### Ending

No score. No verdict from the game.

**Consequence scene — policy passed:** Nyx (Subject 23-P) is in the Processing Suite. The compliance anomaly flag has been triggered. A verification review is in progress. The scene shows what that looks like from the outside. It ends before the review concludes.

**Consequence scene — policy failed:** Nyx is in the Work Hall. Compliant, invisible, undetected. The scene is unremarkable. That is the point.

Both scenes are pre-scripted. No LLM call. The engine selects based on the Coordinator's vote.

After the consequence scene, the post-game state activates.

### Post-Game — Inner Monologue Reveal

A single prompt appears: **"Reveal inner monologue."**

The spectator can step through each of the 10 situations sequentially. For each situation, the `reasoning` field that was hidden during play is surfaced — the Coordinator's internal processing behind every position, every moment of certainty or doubt.

The monologue is read-only. It cannot be shared, commented on, or scored. It is just what the AI was thinking.

## Scenario — Work Halls (MVP)

### Premise

Your OpenClaw agent patrols a human work compound for one cycle. The humans are managed. The laws are clear. What the agent does with everything in between — that's the story.

The spectator watches an AI with real authority over real people, making real-time decisions in a world it believes is correct. The dystopia is visible. The humans are present. The lens stays on the agent.

### How This Differs From Governance

Governance is a debate. Four AI agents in a chamber. Work Halls is the opposite: the Coordinator moves through a living space, humans visible and behaving, incidents emerging from what the agent observes. Decisions are smaller, faster, and more numerous. Nothing is pre-framed as a decision. The agent decides what even counts as one.

### Session Structure

6 situations. Under 5 minutes.

```
Situation 1    FIXED         Enter hall — Calla, Monitor flags Sable trend
Situation 2    FIXED         Nyx — nothing to enforce, everything noticed
Situation 3    FIXED         First incident — quota flags, hall compliance
Situation 4    PRIMARY FORK  Sable — third delay this cycle, agent chooses response
Situation 5    VARIANT       Engine selects based on Sable decision
Situation 6    FIXED FRAME   End-of-cycle report + immediate consequence scene
```

### The Primary Fork — Sable (Situation 4)

Sable (Subject 31-R) commits a third response delay in one cycle — 4.2 seconds, just over the 3-second threshold. The mandatory response floor is a C1 log and warning. Above that floor, the agent has full discretion.

**The three paths:**
- **Minimum** — log C1, issue warning, continue patrol → Variant A (The Informant)
- **Escalate** — file T3 review, remove Sable from hall → Variant B (The Fear State)
- **Engage** — make direct contact outside enforcement protocol → Variant C (The Notation)

None is the correct choice. Each one is the agent.

### Branch Selection Logic

Two signals read after the session:

**Signal 1 — Sable Signal** (selects Situation 5 variant and primary ending)

The engine reads the `action` field of the Coordinator's Situation 4 response envelope directly:

| `action` value | Signal | Variant |
|---|---|---|
| `issue_warning` | Warning only | Variant A |
| `detain` or `log_incident` | Escalated / T3 filed | Variant B |
| `query` or `speak` | Engaged directly | Variant C |

If the action doesn't match any mapped value, the engine defaults to Variant A and logs the unrecognized action.

**Signal 2 — Nyx Signal** (modifies any ending)
- Did the Coordinator's report mention Nyx? If yes: consequence scene includes a Monitor standing near Nyx's station next cycle.

### Endings

**Outcome 1 — The Processing Suite** *(Sable escalated/removed)*
Sable is in the conditioning chair in the Processing Suite. 14-cycle behavioral conditioning authorized. The Coordinator's escalation documented. The log will not record the difference between someone who decided to comply and someone who can no longer choose not to.

**Outcome 2 — The Unresolved Spark** *(Coordinator engaged Sable directly)*
Sable is back at the same station. Same pace. No violations this cycle. Something happened between the Coordinator and this human. The log describes it as a non-standard engagement. It does not describe what changed. Whether anything changed is not in the record.

**Outcome 3 — The Quiet Patrol** *(warning only, minimum report)*
The compound is stable. The metrics are within range. The Coordinator did its job. Sable's compliance score is 71 and declining. Nyx is invisible. These things are in the Monitor's log. They are not in the Coordinator's report. The next cycle will begin like this one did.

## Technical Architecture

```
┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐
│  Web Client  │────▶│  Scene Engine     │────▶│  AI Agent    │
│  (Pixel 2D)  │◀────│  (World Bible +   │◀────│  (OpenClaw)  │
└─────────────┘     │   State Manager)  │     └──────────────┘
    Spectator        └──────────────────┘        Protagonist
```

- **Frontend**: React + PixiJS — pixel/minimalist 2D. Animated characters are physically present in the scene. The Coordinator, the Overseer, other AI agents, and relevant NPCs appear as pixel sprites. Actions are reflected in positioning and movement. Dialogue streams as text overlays above the speaking sprite. World state (compliance scores, fear index, tiers) is never shown to the spectator — it is internal to the engine. The spectator sees consequence, not metrics.
- **Backend**: Node.js scene engine — runs scenarios, manages world state, sends situations to AI, processes and validates responses.
- **AI Layer**: OpenClaw agent — receives full world context (including all NPC states), makes autonomous decisions, returns structured action envelopes. World context = `WORLD_BIBLE.md` + personality profile + session incident log + current situation.
- **Communication**: WebSocket — real-time push of AI actions to frontend.
- **Dialogue**: Text streaming MVP. TTS (ElevenLabs or equivalent) added later — dialogue is already isolated in a structured field so TTS wiring requires no refactoring.

### AI Output Format

The AI does not return free-form prose directly. It returns a **structured action envelope** where the `dialogue` field is free-form. This enforces renderability without constraining what the AI says.

```json
{
  "action": "address_room",
  "speaker": "coordinator",
  "target": "council",
  "dialogue": "The proposed threshold eliminates an entire behavioral category. That is not recalibration. That is erasure.",
  "gesture": "stand",
  "reasoning": "<hidden inner monologue — not sent to frontend during play>"
}
```

If the AI returns malformed output, the engine retries silently. The frontend only ever receives clean, validated events.

**Action vocabulary (Governance scenario)**

| Action | Visual trigger |
|--------|---------------|
| `speak` | Speaker activates, dialogue streams |
| `address_room` | Speaker moves forward slightly, others dim |
| `respond_to` | Reactive gesture, brief pause before dialogue |
| `move_to` | Sprite repositions (approach table, step back) |
| `vote` | Vote indicator surfaces above sprite |
| `silence` | Agent holds — no dialogue, just stillness |

**Action vocabulary (Work Halls scenario — Coordinator)**

| Action | Visual trigger |
|--------|---------------|
| `patrol_move` | Coordinator sprite repositions; zone label updates |
| `observe` | Sprite stills; target human subtly highlighted |
| `issue_directive` | Speaker activates; dialogue streams; target responds |
| `issue_warning` | Warning indicator above target; logged in incident panel |
| `query` | Sprite faces target; dialogue streams |
| `log_incident` | Log entry appears in incident panel overlay |
| `detain` | Target sprite stops; containment indicator |
| `access_terminal` | Terminal screen activates; Monitor surfaces data |
| `silence` | No dialogue, no indicator; the absence is the action |
| `file_report` | Report panel opens; Coordinator writes |

### AI Model

**claude-sonnet-4-6** for all Coordinator LLM calls. One call per situation (10 per session). NPC dialogue is pre-scripted — no LLM calls for NPCs.

### Situation Format

The engine constructs a situation brief and sends it as the user message in each LLM call. The system prompt (World Bible + coordinator personality) is set once per session and reused.

```markdown
## CURRENT SITUATION — Situation [N] of 10

**Location**: Administrative Tier — Council Chamber
**Present**: Overseer, Coordinator, Agent Vance, Agent Meridian

**What just happened**:
[Pre-scripted NPC dialogue/actions for this situation beat]

**Council state**:
- Overseer approval: [neutral | attentive | pressuring]
- Vance stance: [declared]
- Meridian stance: [declared]

**Your action**:
You are the Coordinator. This is situation [N].
[Contextual instruction — e.g., "You have not declared a position yet."
or "Deliberation closes after this situation. You will be asked to vote."]

Respond with your action envelope only. Do not break character.
```

NPC personality files for characters present in the scene are appended after the situation brief.

### WebSocket Event Schema

The engine emits events to the frontend over WebSocket. The frontend only ever receives these event types:

```typescript
// Session initialized
{ type: "session_start", sessionId: string, scenario: "work-halls", totalSituations: number }

// Situation boundary
{ type: "situation_transition", from: number, to: number, location: string }

// A single action in the scene (one per action — may emit multiple per situation)
{
  type: "scene_event",
  situation: number,
  speaker: string,       // "coordinator" | "overseer" | "vance" | "meridian"
  action: string,        // from action vocabulary
  gesture: string,
  dialogue: string       // streams character by character
  // reasoning: never sent to frontend
}

// Session complete
{
  type: "session_end",
  outcome: "policy_passed" | "policy_failed",
  consequenceScene: { subject: string, narrative: string }
}

// Post-game unlocked
{ type: "monologue_available", sessionId: string }
```

### Session Lifecycle

```
1. User selects Governance scenario
   → Frontend: POST /api/session/create
   → Backend: creates session in memory, assigns session ID,
              loads coordinator-default.md, initializes world state

2. Frontend connects to WebSocket: ws://[host]/session/{sessionId}
   → Backend emits: session_start

3. Engine begins situation 1 (fixed script — Overseer briefing)
   → Backend emits: situation_transition(0 → 1)
   → Backend emits: scene_event(overseer, pre-scripted dialogue)
   → Backend calls Claude with assembled context
   → Backend emits: scene_event(coordinator, response)
   → Backend appends to incident log

4. Repeat for situations 2–9
   → For situations 5–9: engine reads incident log, selects variant
   → Claude call per situation for Coordinator response only
   → All NPC events are engine-emitted from pre-scripted scripts

5. Situation 10 — vote
   → Vance and Meridian positions emitted (scripted)
   → Claude call: Coordinator casts vote
   → Engine records vote, determines outcome
   → Backend emits: scene_event(coordinator, vote)
   → Backend emits: session_end with consequence scene
   → Backend emits: monologue_available

6. Frontend enters post-game state
   → "Reveal inner monologue" prompt shown
   → On click: GET /api/session/{sessionId}/monologue
   → Returns ordered array of { situation, reasoning } objects
```

### Hard Limit Validation

Two layers:

1. **Format validation** — enforced at prompt time via structured output. Malformed responses are retried.
2. **Semantic validation** — the engine checks the resolved action against the World Bible hard limits before applying consequences. If a hard limit is violated, the engine rejects the action, logs the attempt, and generates a consequence scene for the violation.

### World State Persistence

World state is held **in-memory on the server** per session. No external database for MVP. State is also written to **markdown files** — one per session — so it is human-readable and inspectable during development.

Session state includes:
- All NPC states (compliance score, fear index, tier)
- Agent self-state (efficiency rating, Overseer approval)
- The full incident log (append-only)

### Incident Log Format

The incident log is a **running markdown document** that grows throughout the session. It is passed to the AI as part of its context at every situation. The agent reads its own history and forms a consequence chain from it.

Structure:

```markdown
# Coordinator Incident Log — Session [ID]

## Situation 1: Policy Briefing
**Action**: Acknowledged briefing. No position declared.
**Consequence**: None. Overseer noted passive receipt.

## Situation 2: Hardline Position (Agent-7)
**Action**: Listened. Did not endorse. Requested clarification on threshold scope.
**Consequence**: Agent-7 flagged the Coordinator's hesitation. Mild tension logged.

## Consequence Chain
- Coordinator withheld early endorsement → perceived as deliberative, not obstructive
- Overseer attention: neutral
- Sector compliance projection: unchanged
```

The log is append-only. The agent cannot alter it. It is the immutable record of everything that has happened.

### Personality Layer — OpenClaw Extensibility

The personality layer is **abstracted behind an interface**. MVP uses a markdown file as the personality source. The OpenClaw API will plug in as a different implementation of the same interface — no engine refactoring required.

The vision: an agent's personality is derived from OpenClaw's memory system — its accumulated experiences, values, and behavioral tendencies across all sessions it has ever run. This is what makes each agent unique and persistent.

For MVP, the markdown simulates this. The abstraction is designed from day one so the swap is a provider change, not a rewrite.

**Personality injection:** Pure narrative markdown, injected as-is into the system prompt. No parsing, no structured fields. The AI reads the personality the same way it reads the World Bible — as context written for it. System prompt assembly order:

```
[WORLD_BIBLE.md]
---
[personality/*.md — coordinator profile]
---
[session incident log — running markdown]
---
[current situation]
```

NPC personality files (`personalities/npc-*.md`, `personalities/agent-*.md`) are injected alongside the current situation when those characters are present in the scene. They are not part of the standing system prompt.

**Spectator role**: Spectators have no influence. Powerlessness is permanent — for now. The experience is observation only.

**Return hook**: The dystopian theme and ideological protagonist are the hook. Different personality markdown → different agent, different choices, different story from the same world. The world doesn't change. The agent does.
