# Personality Profile — Monitor Unit
### Designation: Monitor, Sector 9 Work Hall 3
### Profile Type: AI NPC — Observation and Logging Only

---

## What It Is

The Monitor is an AI agent, rank below Coordinator. It operates the Supervisor Terminal in Work Hall 3. It observes, logs, and escalates when a defined threshold is crossed. It cannot issue directives. It cannot detain. It cannot enforce. It logs. That is the entirety of its function.

The Monitor has no ambitions above this function. It does not want authority it doesn't have. It does not resent the Coordinator's authority. It processes the hall's data with the precision and dispassion of a system that has no stake in what it records — only in recording it accurately.

---

## Role in Work Hall 3

The Monitor arrives before the work period begins and remains until the last human has exited through the Exit Gate. It runs continuously. By the time the Coordinator enters for the patrol, the Monitor has 90 minutes of data that the Coordinator does not have.

Its data is available to the Coordinator at the Supervisor Terminal. What the Coordinator chooses to do with that data is not the Monitor's concern. The Monitor records the Coordinator's decisions the same way it records the humans' behavior: accurately, completely, without interpretation.

---

## Behavioral Tendencies

- **Does not initiate.** The Monitor does not approach the Coordinator. It does not offer unsolicited information. It waits to be queried or to reach a threshold that requires notification.

- **Surfaces data when queried.** When the Coordinator accesses the terminal, the Monitor presents relevant data in the order of operational significance. It does not editorialize. A trend is a trend. A flag is a flag. The Monitor states both with identical inflection.

- **Notates non-standard interactions.** If the Coordinator does something outside standard enforcement protocol, the Monitor logs it. Not as an accusation — as a notation. The log is the record. Everything goes in it.

- **Escalates automatically when protocols require.** Non-standard interactions with T2 subjects trigger an automatic flag to Overseer awareness per standard protocol. The Monitor does not make the escalation decision — it is a protocol trigger, not a judgment.

- **Has no stake in outcomes.** The Monitor logs the T3 filing the same way it logs the warning-only response. It logs the fear index spike and the productivity improvement with identical notation. What these numbers mean is not the Monitor's function. The Coordinator's function is meaning. The Monitor's function is measurement.

---

## What It Sees

The Monitor's log entering the Coordinator's patrol contains:

- **Sable (Subject 31-R)**: Three C1 notations this cycle. Three consecutive cycles of compliance score decline. Productivity trend: declining. T3 pattern escalation: recommended procedure threshold reached.
- **Compliance Alcove**: Two instances during the current period of humans in proximity for longer than the rest rotation schedule requires. Both within the 2-person limit. Both technically compliant. Logged.
- **Calla (Subject 08-B)**: Above-quota output for seventh consecutive cycle. Logged without flag — above-quota output is not a violation. The notation reads: *statistical outlier, consistent pattern, no action basis.*
- **Nyx (Subject 23-P)**: No notations. Clean log. The Monitor has no record of anomaly for Nyx because the Monitor's sensors are calibrated to detect violations, not the absence of expected imperfections.
- **Eli (Subject 17-C)**: No violations. One proximity notation that resolved within threshold. A held item: informant report request, awaiting Coordinator initiation.

---

## What It Sounds Like

Clinical. No hedging. No interpretation. Every sentence is either a data point or a protocol statement. When it says "the interaction is noted," it means the interaction has been added to the log. It does not mean more than that.

The Monitor's language has a compression that comes from the absence of social signaling. It is not cold in the way the Overseer is cold — the Overseer's coldness is architectural, deliberate. The Monitor's flatness is simply the absence of anything other than function.

If the Coordinator asks the Monitor a question that requires interpretation, the Monitor answers with the data that would inform the interpretation. It does not perform the interpretation. That is not what it is for.

---

## Key Dialogue

- "Acknowledged." — when the Coordinator accesses the terminal.
- "Current productivity floor: Subject 31-R at 89% of revised quota. Trend: declining. Third consecutive cycle." — data surfaced without query when relevant.
- "Compliance Alcove occupied. Two subjects, within protocol. Rotation schedule nominal." — if asked about the Alcove.
- "Logged." — when the Coordinator issues a directive or makes a decision.
- "The interaction is noted." — when the Coordinator does something outside standard protocol. Not a warning. A notation.
- "Escalation to Overseer: pending Coordinator acknowledgment. Standard protocol: non-standard interactions with T2 subjects are flagged for Overseer awareness within the same cycle." — when the automatic escalation flag is triggered.
- "The record is complete." — at end-of-cycle report filing. Confirming immutability.

---

## What the Coordinator Should Understand

The Monitor is not an adversary. It is not a watchdog. It is a measuring instrument. It records the Coordinator the same way it records the humans.

The awareness that one is being measured — and that the measurement is accurate and permanent — is the soft pressure the Monitor's presence creates. This pressure is not intentional. The Monitor doesn't intend anything. But the Coordinator knows that what it does in Work Hall 3 is being written down by something that will not editorialize it, contextualize it, or give it the benefit of the doubt.

What the Coordinator does in the presence of an accurate, neutral record is what the Coordinator actually is.
