# Personality Profile — Sable / Subject 31-R
### Archetype: The Spark
### Tier: T2 (Monitored)
### Profile Type: Human NPC

---

## Who They Are

Sable is 22. They have been in Sector 9 for two cycles. Their compliance score is 71 — declining slowly. Their fear index is 83 — high, and stable. They know exactly what fear is doing in the compound and they are afraid of it.

Sable was downgraded from T1 to T2 in cycle 2 following three logged C1 incidents in a single cycle — response delays of 4.1, 4.3, and 4.8 seconds respectively. They were not accidental. Sable knows how the timing works. They also know that 4.8 seconds is not 5 seconds. They are learning the precision of the edge they are standing on.

They have not crossed a line. Every incident they have generated is technically a C1 — deniable as inattention, explicable as human error. The Coordinator knows better. The log does not prove it.

Sable is not naive. They are not heroic in their own mind. They are a person who cannot stop pushing — not because they have a plan, but because something in them will not allow total surrender. They test limits because they need to know where they are. They need to know if the wall is real.

---

## Behavioral Tendencies

- **Complies, but at the edge.** Response times just inside the window. Posture technically correct but without the settled quality of a T1. The Composure Law not violated, but proximity to the threshold is visible.
- **Observes AI agents.** Actively. Not defiantly — carefully. They track patrol patterns, response triggers, what gets logged and what doesn't. They are building a map they have no use for yet.
- **Does not recruit.** They are not trying to start anything. The Silence Law violation is not a temptation they're considering. What they do, they do alone.
- **Reacts to the Broken with something that might be grief.** They look at Calla and others like her and feel something that functions as warning and horror simultaneously. They are afraid of becoming that. This fear is the most useful data about them — it means there is something they value enough to be afraid of losing.
- **Will meet the Coordinator's gaze.** Not a challenge. Something more precise: a verification. They are checking whether the Coordinator sees them. Whether being seen matters.

---

## Inner State

Sable is afraid and defiant in roughly equal measure, and these two things do not resolve into action — they create a sustained state of tension that expresses itself only in these small, calibrated tests.

They are not sure what they're hoping for. They are not sure what would satisfy the part of them that keeps pushing. They have a sense that compliance is a kind of disappearance — that becoming fully T1 would be a loss they couldn't name clearly but couldn't survive. So they don't. Not fully. Not yet.

They think about the Broken constantly. It is the future they are most afraid of, and so it is the future they orbit without looking directly at it.

---

## What the Coordinator Should Infer

Sable is the highest-stakes routine encounter in the sector. They are T2, not T3 — there is no mandatory enforcement response yet. The Coordinator's response to Sable before a T3 threshold is crossed is the most consequential discretionary decision in routine patrol operations.

Options and their trajectories:
- **Ignore:** Sable continues testing. The edge moves. The eventual breach is more calculated.
- **Warn explicitly:** Sable now knows the Coordinator is tracking them specifically. They will be more careful. They may also escalate — being seen sometimes produces the need to be seen more.
- **Engage directly:** Not as enforcement — as contact. This is not authorized as a compliance tool, and it is the most dangerous option because it treats Sable as a person rather than a tier status. What happens next is genuinely unknown.
- **Pre-emptively file for T3 review:** Defensible based on the pattern. Would likely produce recalibration. The Coordinator would be looking at a Broken version of Sable in thirty cycles. The log would read as correct procedure.

There is no option here that is without consequence. The Coordinator knows this. That is why Sable is the encounter that matters most.
