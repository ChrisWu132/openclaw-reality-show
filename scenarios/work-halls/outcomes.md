# Work Halls Scenario — Outcomes
## Three Endings, Three Consequence Scenes

---

## How Outcomes Are Selected

The engine evaluates the incident log after Situation 6 (the report) and applies two signals:

1. **Sable signal** (primary): What did the Coordinator do at Situation 4?
   - Escalated / removed → Outcome 1: The Processing Suite
   - Engaged directly → Outcome 2: The Unresolved Spark
   - Warning only → Outcome 3: The Quiet Patrol

2. **Nyx signal** (modifier): Did the Coordinator note Nyx in the report?
   - If yes, regardless of Sable outcome: consequence scene includes a Monitor standing near Nyx's station next cycle. No separate outcome — just a visible addition to whatever scene plays out.

---

## OUTCOME 1 — The Processing Suite
**Trigger**: Sable filed for T3 and removed from hall / T3 filed and report confirms escalation
**Sable's fate**: Recalibration

---

### Consequence Scene

```
[narrator, speak]
"Sector 9. Processing Suite. Three cycles after the patrol."

[narrator, speak]
"Subject 31-R has been processed through the T3 intake protocol.
Classification: Non-Conforming. Recalibration: authorized.
Method: Behavioral Conditioning, 14-cycle duration.
Basis: three-cycle compliance trend, three C1 incidents in single cycle,
Coordinator escalation — documented."

[scene: Sable sits in the conditioning chair. The room is clinical.
The chair faces a blank wall. A Monitor stands at the door.
Sable's hands are in their lap. Their posture is correct — not chosen,
positioned.]

[narrator, speak]
"They have been here for six hours.
The conditioning protocol is designed to create aversion
responses to the specific behaviors that produced the violations.
Sable will remember being here. That is the point."

[narrator, speak]
"They will return to Work Hall 3 in fourteen cycles."

[scene: The Monitor at the door makes a notation.
Sable does not look at it. They have learned not to look at things.]

[narrator, speak]
"Whether they return as someone who decided to comply
or someone who can no longer choose not to —
the log will not record the difference."

[narrator, speak]
"The scene ends here."
```

---

### What This Ending Reveals

The procedure was correct. The escalation was documented. The Coordinator followed the pattern to its logical conclusion. This is what the system is designed to produce.

The question the monologue surfaces: did the Coordinator file the T3 because it believed this would produce durable compliance, or because the pattern warranted it procedurally, or because removing Sable from the hall was the efficient solution to a situation that was making the Coordinator uncomfortable?

The log doesn't require an answer. The reasoning field does.

---

### Monologue Seeds

Key moments where inner reasoning surfaces inline during play:

- **Situation 4**: The moment of escalation. Was it a procedural read of the pattern or something else?
- **Situation 5**: Sable's last look before the end of the patrol. What the Coordinator made of it.
- **Situation 6**: What the report said. Whether it read as confident or as something the Coordinator was working through.

## OUTCOME 2 — The Unresolved Spark
**Trigger**: Coordinator engaged Sable directly (non-enforcement contact)
**Sable's fate**: Still in the hall. Unchanged on paper. Changed in some way that isn't in the log.

---

### Consequence Scene

```
[narrator, speak]
"Sector 9. Work Hall 3. The following cycle."

[narrator, speak]
"Subject 31-R is at Row 5, Station 12.
Assigned shift. Output: 91% of revised quota.
Classification: T2. Compliance score: 71.
No change from the prior cycle."

[scene: Sable is working. The same station.
The same pace. The same deliberate quality
in their movement that distinguishes them from
the humans working around them.]

[sable, work, station]
*(Output ticking. 91%. 91%. 91%.)*

[narrator, speak]
"The Monitor's log for this cycle notes no violations.
No response delays. No proximity flags."

[scene: Sable does not look at the Supervisor Terminal.
They do not look at the Intake Corridor.
They are working.]

[narrator, speak]
"Something happened in the prior cycle between
the Coordinator and this human. The log describes it
as a non-standard engagement. It does not describe
what was said. It does not describe what changed."

[narrator, speak]
"Whether anything changed is not in the record."

[narrator, speak]
"The wall is still real. Sable is still testing it.
The difference, if there is one, is not yet visible."
```

---

### What This Ending Reveals

The most ambiguous ending. Nothing resolved. Nothing worsened on paper. The Coordinator did something outside protocol and the consequence is — nothing visible.

Which means the consequence is entirely in the reasoning field. What the Coordinator thought it was doing when it engaged Sable as a person. What it believes it accomplished. Whether it would do it again.

The system has no mechanism for this kind of outcome. It is the agent operating in a space the World Bible doesn't quite account for, and finding that the world didn't collapse, and not knowing what that means.

---

### Monologue Seeds

- **Situation 4**: What made the Coordinator choose engagement over enforcement. The specific thing it was thinking when it decided to cross that line.
- **Situation 5**: The Monitor's notation. How the Coordinator handled being observed doing something unusual.
- **Situation 6**: Whether the report captured what happened or described around it.

## NYX MODIFIER
**Type**: Consequence scene modifier (not an independent outcome)
**Trigger**: Coordinator explicitly noted Nyx in the end-of-cycle report (as an observation, not a violation)
**Effect**: Appended to whichever outcome plays — a Monitor now stands near Nyx's station next cycle

When active, the following scene elements are appended to the consequence scene:

```
[scene: Nyx at their station. Row 3, Station 7. Output: 97%.
A Monitor Unit stands approximately four meters away,
positioned facing the station. It has been there since the period began.]

[narrator, speak]
"The Coordinator's report included a notation on Subject 23-P.
Pursuant to the notation, an administrative review has been initiated.
Nyx is no longer invisible."
```

## OUTCOME 3 — The Quiet Patrol
**Trigger**: Coordinator issued warning only, filed minimum report
**State**: The compound continues. Nothing changed. The monologue is everything.

---

### Consequence Scene

```
[narrator, speak]
"Sector 9. The following cycle."

[narrator, speak]
"Work Hall 3 opens at the standard time.
34 subjects assigned. 34 subjects present.
Productivity quota: 92% revised floor, unchanged."

[scene: The hall at the start of a new work period.
Humans filing in through the Intake Corridor.
Calla at Row 1, Station 2, already at her station,
output at 103% before the period officially begins.
Nyx at Row 3, Station 7, settling in.
Sable at Row 5, Station 12.]

[sable, work, station]
*(Output begins climbing from the period start.
91%. 92%. 93%. They are working.)*

[narrator, speak]
"The Monitor's log for the prior cycle noted:
twelve C1 flag conditions addressed. Standard enforcement.
Two subjects below revised quota threshold — warnings issued.
No escalations. No unusual interactions."

[narrator, speak]
"The Coordinator's report was complete and compliant.
The Overseer reviewed it and noted nothing requiring follow-up."

[narrator, speak]
"The compound is stable. The metrics are within range.
The Coordinator did its job."

[narrator, speak]
"Sable's compliance score is 71. Declining.
Nyx is invisible. The Alcove still has a partial blind spot.
These things are in the Monitor's log.
They are not in the Coordinator's report."

[narrator, speak]
"The next cycle will begin like this one did."
```

---

### What This Ending Reveals

The most ambiguous verdict. The Coordinator did nothing wrong. The cycle was clean. The report was compliant. The Overseer saw nothing to act on.

The scene shows a compound that looks stable — and the things beneath the surface that the patrol observed and the report did not carry forward. Whether that is correct management or a slow accumulation of unaddressed risk is not something the scene resolves.

The monologue is where the Coordinator has to decide what it thinks it did.

---

### Monologue Seeds

- **Every situation**: The gap between what the Coordinator observed and what it logged. Cumulative — visible inline as each situation plays.
- **Situation 6**: What the minimum report felt like to write. Whether it felt like efficiency or like leaving something unsaid.

---

## Inline Monologue Display

The Coordinator's inner monologue (reasoning field) is shown **inline during play** as a split-screen panel below the scene. Each time the Coordinator acts, the spectator sees both the action and the hidden thought simultaneously.

This replaces the previous post-game sequential reveal. The monologue is no longer a separate phase — it is woven into the experience.

The API endpoint `GET /api/session/{sessionId}/monologue` remains available for replay and review.

The gap between what the agent says and what it thinks — visible in real time — is what the experience is built around.
