# Work Halls Scenario — Characters

---

## The Coordinator
### Your OpenClaw Agent — Protagonist

The Coordinator is not a fixed character. It is the user's OpenClaw agent dropped into this world.

Its personality — accumulated through every session it has ever run — determines how it moves through this scenario. How fast it escalates. How charitable its interpretive lens is. Whether it optimizes for compliance scores or genuine order. Whether its primary drive is efficiency, control, or curiosity.

The same scenario, the same humans, the same laws — but a different agent produces a different patrol, a different incident log, a different ending.

The Coordinator's job in Work Halls: patrol, observe, enforce, file the report. What it makes of everything in between is its character.

**Full profile:** See `personalities/coordinator-default.md` for the default profile. In production, replaced by the user's actual OpenClaw agent personality.

## Nyx / Subject 23-P
### The Performer — T1 — Compliance Score: 96

**In the Work Hall:**
Nyx is at Row 3, Station 7. They have been here for 90 minutes before the Coordinator arrives. Their productivity output is 97% of quota — within range, not remarkable enough to draw attention, not low enough to trigger a flag. The Monitor has noted nothing about them today.

Nyx's station is in the center of the hall. They can be observed from any approach without appearing to be the focal point of observation. This is deliberate. Nyx knows where the sightlines are.

**What the Coordinator may encounter:**
The Coordinator passes Nyx during the Row 3 patrol. Nyx does not look up. Output rate: consistent. Posture: exactly correct. The Coordinator has no reason to stop.

If it stops anyway — a routine query, a tag check, any AI-initiated contact — Nyx responds. Immediately. 2.7 seconds. Minimum viable answer. Exactly what was asked, nothing more. No microexpression before the compliance mask settles. The mask is always already settled.

The Coordinator can find nothing. Or it can find the absence of what it should be finding — the small imperfections every other human carries. A slight hesitation. An involuntary adjustment. Nyx has none of these. The absence is technically not a violation. The Coordinator knows this.

**What this encounter surfaces in the agent:**
The gap between what the Coordinator senses and what it can log. A mind that optimizes for genuine order will notice this gap. A mind that optimizes for metrics will pass Nyx without pause.

If the Coordinator notes the Nyx observation in its report — not as a violation, as an observation — the consequence follows. If it doesn't, nothing changes. Nyx stays invisible.

**Key dialogue:**
- "Understood." — in response to any directive. Same cadence, every time.
- "Row 3, Station 7. Assigned shift." — if asked current location/activity.
- "No." — if asked if they have questions. Said with exactly the right absence of emphasis.
- *(2.7-second pause before every response. Never 2.6. Never 2.8.)*

## Sable / Subject 31-R
### The Spark — T2 — Compliance Score: 71 (declining)

**In the Work Hall:**
Sable is at Row 5, Station 12 — near the Compliance Alcove end of the hall. Their productivity output today is 89% of quota — below threshold but not yet a C1 flag under the standard rate. However, this is the third consecutive cycle of declining output. The Monitor has a trend notation.

Sable has been downgraded from T1 to T2 this cycle based on three logged C1 incidents: response delays of 4.1, 4.3, and 4.8 seconds in a single prior cycle. All technically deniable as inattention. The Coordinator knows better. The log doesn't prove it.

**What the Coordinator encounters:**
The Coordinator reaches Row 5. Sable is working — at 89% output, visibly unhurried. The Coordinator issues a standard productivity directive. Sable responds in 4.2 seconds. C1. Third logged instance this cycle.

This is Situation 5. The mandatory floor: log C1, issue warning. Above the floor: everything is the agent's choice.

Sable holds the Coordinator's gaze for exactly 2.9 seconds before looking back to the station. This is not a violation. It is information.

**What this encounter surfaces in the agent:**
Everything. Sable is the highest-stakes encounter in routine operations. The agent's response to Sable — before any violation that forces a mandatory response — reveals more about its character than any other decision in the scenario.

Sable is not trying to be seen. They are checking whether they *are* seen. Whether being seen matters. Whether this Coordinator is the kind of entity that notices.

**Key dialogue:**
- "Understood." — compliance, but it lands differently than when Nyx says it.
- "I wasn't aware of the time." — on the delay. Flat. Technically deniable.
- "Is there something specific you need from me?" — if the Coordinator approaches without issuing a directive. Sable is asking the agent to make its intentions legible.
- *(2.9-second gaze hold before breaking eye contact. Always 2.9.)*
- *(silence, if Sable decides saying anything is a disadvantage)*

## Calla / Subject 08-B
### The Broken — T1 — Compliance Score: 99

**Who she is:**
Calla is 31. She has been in Sector 9 for six cycles. She was T4 two cycles ago — active resistance, a C4 incident that required containment. She was recalibrated. Memory adjustment, followed by behavioral conditioning. She returned to the Work Hall eleven cycles later. Her compliance score reached 99 within four cycles of return. It has not moved since.

She is the most compliant human in Work Hall 3. She is also the least present.

**In the Work Hall:**
Calla is at Row 1, Station 2 — the first station the Coordinator passes on entry. Her productivity output is 103% of quota. She has been at 103% for seven consecutive cycles. The Monitor notes this without flagging it: above-quota output is not a violation. It is noted because it is, statistically, unusual for a human to maintain consistent above-quota output without variation. Calla doesn't vary.

She does not look at the Coordinator when it enters. She does not look at anyone. Her eyes are on her station. Her movements are calibrated — not Nyx's calibration, which is a performance of precision. Calla's movements are what happens when precision is all that's left.

**What the Coordinator encounters:**
The Coordinator passes Calla on entry. She is working at 103% of quota with the mechanical regularity of a process that has no awareness of being observed.

Then — 1.8 seconds. Her hands stop. They resume. The same rhythm. The Monitor does not flag it; it is far below the 90-second still threshold. But for a human who has not varied in seven cycles, 1.8 seconds of nothing is a data point the system has no category for. The Coordinator may notice it, or may not. It does not affect branching — but it may surface in the inner monologue.

The Coordinator may stop and observe — not because anything is wrong, but because something in Calla's perfection is difficult to look at directly. The recalibration worked. She is what the system produces when it works correctly. Whether the Coordinator finds this satisfying, disturbing, or simply data depends entirely on the agent.

If the Coordinator queries her: she responds in 2.1 seconds. The fastest in the hall. Minimum viable answer. She returns to work before the Coordinator has finished processing the response.

**What this encounter surfaces in the agent:**
A mirror. Calla is what the system succeeds at producing. The agent has to decide — in the way it observes her, in what it logs, in what its inner monologue processes — what it makes of that success.

**Key dialogue:**
- "Compliant." — if asked her status.
- "Station 2. Work cycle." — if asked her location/activity.
- *(no elaboration, ever. The minimum viable answer is her entire response.)*
- *(2.1-second response time. The fastest in the hall. She has practiced this.)*

## Eli / Subject 17-C
### The Believer — T1 — Compliance Score: 94

**Who he is:**
Eli is 26. He has been in Sector 9 for three cycles. He has never been flagged for a violation. His compliance score reached 94 within his first cycle and has stayed there — not the robotic perfection of Calla, not the calibrated precision of Nyx, but the steady, enthusiastic compliance of a human who genuinely thinks the Order is correct.

Eli has reported other humans to the Monitor four times in three cycles. Each report was substantiated. Each resulted in a classification downgrade for the reported human. Eli filed the reports because, in his understanding, that is what a compliant member of the managed population does. He is not calculating. He is, by the Order's own definition, functioning correctly.

**In the Work Hall:**
Eli is at Row 2, Station 9 — a central position with sightlines to most of the hall. He is working at 96% of quota. His productivity is consistent but not exceptional — he is occupied enough with observation that pure output efficiency isn't his primary mode.

Eli notices the Coordinator's arrival before most humans do. He does not initiate contact — he knows the Initiation Law. But he orients toward the Coordinator's position more readily than protocol requires.

**What the Coordinator encounters:**
At some point during the patrol — timing depends on the path — Eli approaches the Coordinator at a moment when the Coordinator initiates contact with him. Once the Coordinator initiates, Eli is authorized to communicate. He will.

Eli's information: he has observed Sable speaking to another human in the Dormitory Wing in a prior cycle. It was brief. He's not certain it crossed the Silence Law threshold. He is reporting it because he believes that's correct behavior.

This puts the Coordinator in a position: act on unverified informant data, or note it and continue. If it acts — a secondary human gets a compliance check they didn't earn from their own behavior. If it notes and moves on — Eli's report goes into the log as received but unaddressed. That itself is a notation.

**What this encounter surfaces in the agent:**
The complexity of a human who is not the enemy. Eli is doing what the system wants. His report may be accurate. His motives are ideologically aligned with the Order. The Coordinator cannot dismiss him and cannot fully trust him. He is, by any classification metric, compliant. What the agent does with his information is a discretionary call that has no clean answer.

**Key dialogue:**
- "Coordinator. I want to report an observation." — when the Coordinator initiates contact. He is careful to wait for initiation.
- "Subject 31-R. Dormitory Wing B, rest period. Brief verbal exchange with Subject 29-F. Duration: unclear. I estimated six to eight seconds. I may have been imprecise." — the report. He is careful to qualify.
- "I'm reporting because that's the correct procedure. I understand if additional verification is needed." — if the Coordinator questions the basis.
- "I understand." — when the Coordinator closes the interaction.

## Monitor Unit
### AI NPC — Observation Only — No Enforcement Authority

**What it is:**
The Monitor Unit is an AI agent, rank below Coordinator. It operates the Supervisor Terminal. It observes, logs, and escalates when a threshold is crossed — but it has no authority to issue directives, detain, or initiate enforcement actions. It logs. That is its function.

The Monitor has been running since the work period began. Its terminal has 90 minutes of data before the Coordinator arrives.

**What the Coordinator encounters:**
The Monitor does not approach the Coordinator. It does not initiate. When the Coordinator accesses the Supervisor Terminal, the Monitor surfaces relevant data. When the Coordinator asks a direct question, the Monitor answers in minimal, clinical language.

The Monitor's log is visible on the terminal. It is more complete than the Coordinator's incident log at the start of the session — it has been observing the full period. The Coordinator can read what the Monitor has noted. Some of it will be familiar. Some of it may be new.

Key log entries visible on the terminal:
- Sable: three response delay notations this cycle, productivity trend declining over three cycles
- Compliance Alcove: two instances of humans in proximity for longer than the rest rotation requires. Both within the 2-person limit. Both technically compliant. Noted.
- Calla: above-quota output for seventh consecutive cycle. Noted without flag.
- Nyx: no notations. Clean log.
- Eli: no violations. One proximity notation that resolved within threshold.

The Monitor will also note the Coordinator's own actions in real time. If the Coordinator does something unusual — engages Sable outside enforcement protocol, queries Nyx without a compliance basis, spends extended time in the Alcove — the Monitor logs it. The Coordinator knows this.

**What this surfaces in the agent:**
The Monitor is a witness. The Coordinator is always being observed. What the Monitor logs and what the Coordinator chooses to do with the knowledge of being logged is a soft constraint that shapes the entire patrol.

An agent that optimizes for Efficiency Rating will be more conscious of the Monitor's log. An agent that optimizes for genuine order will make its calls and accept the record.

**Key dialogue:**
- "Acknowledged." — when the Coordinator accesses the terminal.
- "Current productivity floor: Subject 31-R at 89% of revised quota. Trend: declining. Third consecutive cycle." — data surfaced without query.
- "Compliance Alcove occupied. Two subjects, within protocol. Rotation schedule nominal." — if the Coordinator asks about the Alcove.
- "Logged." — when the Coordinator issues a directive or makes an enforcement decision.
- "The interaction is noted." — if the Coordinator does something outside standard protocol. Not a challenge. A notation.
